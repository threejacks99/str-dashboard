---
title: "Setting Up Multiple Properties"
category: "Managing Properties"
---

If you run more than one rental, Hostics is built to treat them as a portfolio: each property keeps its own bookings, expenses, and reports, and you can also view everything combined. Here's how to set that up well.

## Add each property first

Before importing any data, add every rental under **Properties → Add Property**. Two reasons to do this up front:

1. Imports match rows to properties by **name**, so the properties need to exist with the right names before a multi-property file can land cleanly.
2. It lets you choose consistent names that match what your booking and expense exports call each rental.

## Name properties to match your data

The single biggest time-saver for a portfolio is naming each property in Hostics exactly as it appears in the **Property** / **Listing** column of your exports. Matching is case-insensitive and ignores surrounding spaces, but is otherwise exact — so "Lakeview Cabin" and "Lakeview Cabin" match, while "Lakeview" and "Lakeview Cabin" don't. Get the names aligned and multi-property imports require no manual mapping at all.

## Importing portfolio-wide files

Channel managers and PM portals often export all your properties in one file. Hostics reads these directly — see [Importing multiple properties at once](/help/multi-property-import) for the recommended file shape and how the name-matching and "create new" steps work.

If your data instead comes as separate per-property files (common when you download each Airbnb listing separately), just upload each one with its property selected at the top of **Add Data**.

## Viewing one property or all of them

Every analytics page — Dashboard, Financials, Bookings — has a property filter at the top. Switch between a single property and **All Properties** to move between a portfolio-wide view and a single-rental deep dive. Looking at each property individually is the only way to see which ones are actually profitable; an all-properties NOI can hide a money-loser behind your winners.

## Plan limits and overage

Your plan caps how many properties you can have:

- **Solo** covers 1 property.
- **Portfolio** includes 10. Beyond that, each additional property adds a small charge (currently $5/month or $50/year), confirmed when you add it and billed from your next renewal.
- **Investor** covers up to 50.
- **Enterprise** is for more than 50 — contact sales.

If you're consistently near or over your limit, switching to annual billing or a higher tier is usually cheaper than stacking overage. See [Billing & Account](/help/billing-account).
